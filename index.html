Bois Forte Band of Chippewa

Located in northern MN near International Falls, MN

  • Nett Lake is one of the largest wild rice producers in the world.
  • The band operates its own radio station, KBFT.
  • Bois Forte members were skilled canoe builders who used local cedar.

Fond du Lac Band of Lake Superior Chippewa Reservation

Established by the LaPointe Treaty of 1854, is one of six Reservations inhabited by members of the Minnesota Chippewa Tribe.

  • Fond du Lac means “bottom of the lake” in French.
  • The band runs the first Tribal-owned solar thermal project in Minnesota.
  • The Fond du Lac Language program has helped grow new Ojibwe speakers.

Grand Portage Band of Lake Superior Chippewa

Is bordered on the north by Canada, on the south and east by Lake Superior and on the west by Grand Portage State Forest.

  • Grand Portage was a major hub for the fur trade in the 1700s.
  • The 8.5-mile “Grand Portage” trail was a key route for voyageurs.
  • Mount Josephine sits within the reservation and offers views of Isle Royale.

Leech Lake Band of Ojibwe

Their flag represents nature, strength, sovereignty, education, and cultural identity.

  • Leech Lake is home to the largest lake in the reservation system.
  • The band has one of the strongest youth drum and dance circles in the region.
  • The reservation has more lakes than any other U.S. reservation.

Lower Sioux Indian Community

Is located in south central Minnesota in Redwood County, approximately two miles south of Morton.

  • The Dakota name is Cansa’yapi.
  • The community runs a cultural center that teaches traditional arts and language.
  • Lower Sioux is building a large Tribal-run early childhood education center.

Mille Lacs Band of Ojibwe

Our homelands are comprised of three Districts throughout East Central Minnesota, from our 1855 Mille Lacs Reservation east to the St. Croix River and north to Rice Lake and Sandy Lake.

  • The band operates its own community TV and radio broadcast center.
  • Mille Lacs Ojibwe culture includes strong birch bark craft traditions.
  • The band leads major efforts in Tribal sovereignty and treaty rights cases.

Prairie Island Indian Community

Our members descend from the Bdewakantunwan (Mdewakanton) Band of Eastern Dakota. We are the Dakota Oyate (people), are also commonly referred to as the Minnesota Sioux.

  • The Dakota name is Tinta Winta.
  • The community raises bison as part of cultural and ecological restoration.
  • Prairie Island is known for its skilled Dakota beadwork artists.

Red Lake Band of Chippewa Indians

Red Lake Reservation is owned and occupied entirely by members of the Red Lake Band of Chippewa Indians — the only such reservation in Minnesota.

  • Red Lake is one of the only closed reservations in the U.S. with land held collectively.
  • Red Lake walleye is famous across the Midwest.
  • The Nation has its own independent government system, separate since 1918.

Shakopee Mdewakanton Sioux Community

The reservation was known as the Prior Lake Reservation until its reorganization under the Indian Reorganization Act on November 28, 1969.

  • The community is the largest philanthropic Tribes in the U.S.
  • Mdewakanton means “Dwellers at Spirit Lake.”
  • The Tribe is a leader in green energy and water restoration projects.

Upper Sioux Community

We have always occupied this area bordering the Minnesota River Valley, with the exception of a short period of time in the late 1800's following the U.S./Dakota Conflict of 1862.

  • The Dakota name is Pezihutazizi.
  • The community hosts the annual Pezihutazizi Powwow.
  • Upper Sioux maintains restored prairie that supports traditional plant gathering.

White Earth Nation

Created in 1867 by a treaty between the United States and the Mississippi Band of Chippewa Indians, it is one of seven Chippewa reservations in Minnesota.

  • White Earth is the largest Tribe in Minnesota by enrollment.
  • Ric e harvesting on reservation waters remains a key tradition.
  • The Tribe runs one of the strongest language revitalization programs in the state.
